# Adamantine Rose (Requires Attunement)

A small pin in the shape of a rose designed to be worn on clothing.

Any time the wearer of this rose rolls to heal another creature, they may take the maximum possible value.

In addition, this rose has 5 charges. As an action, the wearer of this rose may expend one charge to cast Regenerate.

The rose regains 1d4 charges each day at dawn.

# Necklace of Life (Requires Attunement)

A vague predecessor to the Adamantine Rose.

A pale adamantine necklace with an amethyst gemstone on the clasp. The necklace has 3 Charges. While wearing it, you can use a bonus action to expend 1 of its Charges to cast the Revivfy spell, or 3 of its Charges to cast the Raise Dead spell. It regains one charge every month at dawn on a holiday related to your deity. In addition, while wearing the necklace, all healing die are rolled with advantage. The wearer learns the following cantrips while attuned to the wristband: Control Flames, Dancing Lights, Light, Mage Hand, Minor Illusion, Mold Earth, Prestidigitation, and Shape Water. The wearer may increase their spellcasting ability by 10, to a maximum of 30.

# Magical Cloak (Requires Attunement)

A fine white silk cloak with golden trim in the form of runes.

Once per day, as an Action, the wearer can throw the cloak (20/60ft range) at an enemy for the purpose of restraining or distracting them. When throwing the cloak, make a ranged attack roll against the target. On a success, the target is restrained until they use their Action to make a DC 20 Strength check to escape. When instead distracting a target, the cloak hovers in midair with the purpose to obscure their field of vision until the target uses an Action to dismiss it (DC 17 Dexterity). A distracted creature has disadvantage on attack rolls and saves relying on Sight, and attack rolls against the creature have advantage. A failed throw of the cloak does not consume the cloak's charge.

The owner of the cloak also may increase their Strength by 6 to a maximum of 26, and gains proficiency in intimidation. If they are already proficient, they instead gain expertise.

A small pair of glasses perfectly fit for your vision. The wearer gains proficiency in perception. If already proficient, it grants expertise instead. If you already have expertise, er... Advantage on Perception checks relying on Sight. In addition, the wearer gains Truesight out to a distance of 300 feet.

# Quill of Spell Twisting (Requires Attunement)

A quill and a vial of green ink. When attuned to, the quill can write in midair. The quill has two charges, both of which regenerate at Dawn each day.

Over an hour, the wearer may change, delete, or add a letter in the name of one of their spells. This hour does not count as a short rest. A spell may not be more than two letters away from the original. After a month after the last change to a spell, the changes revert. If instead the user has a spellbook, the changes last until undone. Spells altered in this way will have their effects, damage, level, casting time, duration, range and similar.

To affect an unwilling target, the target must be incapacitated, paralyzed, or unconscious. Attempting to work on an unconscious target will force a DC 15 Wisdom save to wake up. After the hour, the target must make a Widsom save (DC 30). On a successful save, the target is immune to the effects of the quill for a week. The target will not know their spell(s) were changed unless they inspect (DC 20 Investigation) or cast them.

The amount of charges required to change or add a letter of a spell is equal to the number of letters already changed plus one (for example, a spell with two letters changed requires three charges to modify). The amount of charges to undo a change is equal to the amount of changes currently on the spell. When the quill reaches zero charges, flip a coin. On a tails, the quill disintegrates.

# Silver Crown (Requires Attunement)

A small silver crown This item has five charges, which it regains each day at dawn.

As an action, the wearer may expend one charge to attempt to charm a nearby person, who must succeed on a DC 15 wisdom save to avoid being charmed. On a successful save, the target is immune to the effects of the crown for 24 hours. The charmed target regards the wearer as a trusted friend to be heeded and protected. On a 1, the silver turns to lead and the crown loses its magical properties.
